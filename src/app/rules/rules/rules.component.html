Life As A Mouse

Every fourth generation is cursed to live in interesting times, so interesting in fact that between 50% and 75% of the population does not survive each calamity. It is up to them to make sure something survives into the future. Four generations ago it was the Invasion by savages. Four generations before that was the Flood. Four generations before that was the Earthquake. The calamity that defines this generation has yet to come, but the mice of the County are preparing as best they can to weather what comes and hope their house comes out on top.

In this game you will be running as the head of house for a group of mice. The head of house does not go adventuring, but does have a method of operating that defines their house and the way they operate, similar to the houses in Game of Thrones. While the goal of many houses in GoT is to see who ends up on the Iron Throne, the goal here is to come out the least impacted by the calamity to come.

The cycle of generations are Survivor, Builder, Preparer, Survivor. Your head of house character is going to be from the last generation of Survivors and each of the mice you play in this campaign is going to be from the generation of Survivor that is just now coming of age.

  • Current (Survivor): 3 months old [16 human years]. These are the mice you will be playing and going on adventures with. When your character is created they are celebrating their 3 month birthday to be recorded on their character sheet.
  • Parents (Preparer): 7 months old [35-40 human years].These are the parental figures that are likely to be helpful common NPCs in the game.
  • Grandparents(Builder): 11-12 months old [60-65 human years]. These are figures in the twilight of their working years, the ones that have made it this far tend to be tough, knowledgeable and resourceful. Uncommon NPCs in game, often ones with important names, roles or titles.
  • Great-Grandparents (Survivors): 17+ months old [90+ human years]. These are the living legends. A few tough old birds and deadly old bastards not only survived the last calamity but are too mean, too stubborn, or too driven to die. These are your heads of house.

Mice live short and danger filled lives. 1 month is slightly over 5 mouse years. Most mice "settle down" and have children around 4 months old, however survivor mice tend to be 5 or even 6 months old depending on their individual calamity. The mice of Squeakshire are also much less likely to spread out their births over a series of months so the generations are less gradual than they would be in human communities and most mice are raised semi-communally in the region of their birth, only spending about half of their time with their family unit and the other half of the time with the members of their county in their same generation.

Given the speed of life and the length of recovery, mice that find themselves too often injured might find themselves aged out of the adventuring life all together. A single week of recovery for a mouse is like a human spending a full year of PT to get themselves back on their feet again from a major injury.

Game Mentality

Dice Mice is a tactical OSR (or NSR) inspired game where you play as a game of thrones style house of mice who explore the dangerous world for the good of mouse kind (and the profit of your family and house).

Assumed Competency
Your characters will not have most non-magical equipment marked on their sheet. It is assumed your character is wearing appropriate clothing and armor for their class and the defenses and penalties associated with that are all built into your class. If you have a nonmagical limited resource you are assumed to start with a baseline amount of that resource (Rations, Water, Torches, Arrows etc) listed in your adventuring kit for your class. If you wish to have more than that, you need to explain why you have it on you.
Size Matters (And you are very small)
Size is a massive advantage/disadvantage depending on the situation. Combat with creatures larger than you is likely to be something that works more as a puzzle than as a straight fight as creatures will have a very large advantage in health the larger they get as well as size based damage resistance. Something the size of a large Snake is going to be like fighting a dragon. An encounter with a Lynx or an Owl isn't a fight, its figuring out how many mice escape with their lives. The panicked running legs of a deer are more like tornado touch downs than they are appendages of anything living.
Fair Combat is Failure
If you are in a fair fight something went wrong with the plan. If you are in an unfair fight, something went catastrophically wrong with the plan. A single damage roll can kill one of your characters, especially at low levels, and even surviving involves days or weeks of potential downtime assuming you keep all your limbs/eyes/etc when you take the damage. As such mice are very highly incentivized to not fight fair, to be tactical and only engage with overwhelming advantage.
The House Survives
No matter how many mice you lose, your house survives. It is sad to lose anyone, but there is a reason mice breed so quickly... This also means that the overall feel of your house is vitally important to playing and understanding your characters. In Game of Thrones when Jaime makes a decision he often thinks "What would Jaime do?" but he always has to think "What would a Lannister do?".
Your Fate, Your Dice
The world and its creatures are obstacles that must be overcome. You roll to hit monsters, you roll to make DCs for skill checks, but you also roll to avoid incoming attacks or catastrophes. The only rolls made by the DM are opposing checks (which effectively is a roll I use to set a DC), initiative rolls and hidden checks (which you will often actually roll but only I will see the result).
Magic is Alien and Deadly
Magic is not well understood. Some say it is not understandable. As such no magic comes without a cost (There are no cantrips). Some creatures and desperate mice will risk the consequences of casting spells unaided, however no player class gets this ability as default. The vast majority of casting takes place through the crafting and casting of magic scrolls or through the use of magical tattoos (effectively living scrolls) if the character is a sorcerer.
You Try Anything
A Barbarian can try to read a 7th level spell scroll at level 1 and unleash the magic contained there-in. It is likely to kill them and everyone in the nearby vacinity and have catastrophic consequences, but they can still try.
Recovery Costs
By default mice recover 1 hit point per level per day of recovery, no spells can recover hit points but potions can. Potions are expensive to use, but can shave days or weeks off of recovery time and when used in the wild can be the difference between life and death.
Simple Creatures, Complex World
As you start looking at the classes you will notice that they are very simple. This is intentional. Most of what your character gains is not through class features and levels gained, it is through going into the world and finding things.
Success is Golden
You get experience for getting money for yourself and your house from the wilderness and back to town. The rate of experience is always 1 GP for 1 EXP. Some monsters might have loot or valuable components as parts of their body that can be brought back to town, however usually avoiding fights and sneaking in to get the money is a much better plan. Succeeding at quests or helping the community comes with a GP reward and the corresponding amount of EXP.
Preparation > Recovery
Being prepared for a situation is far more mechanically beneficial than having to deal with something after the fact. For example potions of heroism which grant temporary hit points are much more effective at keeping mice alive than using a potion of healing after they are hurt.
